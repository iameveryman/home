Martin Luther

Father of the Protestant Reformation

  • Born 10 November 1483 in Eisleben, County Mansfield, Holy Roman Empire
  • Ordained to the Roman Catholic priesthood in 1507
  • Taught theology at the University of Wittenberg
  • Composed his Ninety-five Theses and nailed them to the door of All Saints' Church in Wittenberg on 31 October 1517
  • Main objections involved the sale of indulgences
  • Excommunicated from the Roman Catholic Church by Pop Leo X on 3 January 1521
  • Composed the Large and Small Catechisms in 1529 as a means of imparting the basic tenets of Christianity to the congregations
  • Published his German translation of the Bible in 1534
  • Died on 18 February 1546 in Eisleben, from complications stemming from an apoplectic stroke
  • Interred in front of the pulpit of Schlosskirche in Wittenberg

Martin Luther spent the entirety of his life dedicated to his faith and the service of not only those around him but also to the Church. Martin Luther never set out to cause a split in the Church, but to reform it, and in so doing bring it back in line with the teachings of the Bible.
